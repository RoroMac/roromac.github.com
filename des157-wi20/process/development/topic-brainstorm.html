Brainstorm Mapping

Through the brainstorming process, I identified key qualities of current technology, several relevant issues, and generated ideas on how to remedy these problems through the previously identified traits.

Despite the broad nature of technology, I managed to distinguish three primary uses;   measurement, simulation, and communication  . Measurement, in a simple definition, is the recording of information. A more complicated interpretation includes the storing of values, accuracy and range of data, and the functions and calculations of modifying information. The simulation of information involves the presentation, through visuals or physical vibrations. And lastly, communication is the application of technology as a virtual medium for those to spread or gain information.

While I only discussed a small sample of issues, I thought that they had a wide enough variety so that I could explore the possible applications of technology. These issues as seen focused on  public policy awareness, opioid crisis, economic disparity, and mental health  . In discussing these issues, I tried to find several possible causes for why this occurs and how the previously generated traits of technology could help generate possible ideas. Of these ideas, I came up with a select few possible solutions which I would like to research further.

Using  working from home  to help bring jobs to underprivileged and at-risk communities. Could we create jobs for people so that they don’t have to move away from their communities? Could we create virtual economies that don’t require a high degree of education?

Using  crowdsourcing  as a way to help review grant writing and nonprofit proposals. Nonprofits could upload their documentation onto a website and receive feedback and edits so that they can perform better. Additionally, this resource could be used by the general public for awareness of the resources that they provide.

Additionally, as an unexpected conversation forum, I would like to propose a  virtual bus-stop  . This would act similar to a google search engine however with each search, the users get transported to a chat forum with several random members. These members are held there for an indeterminate amount of time from 30-seconds to several minutes. Users are unnamed, random, and from international audiences. Hopefully the shared space and anonymity could help instigate conversations and experiences.

While I’m not too sure if any of these ideas are worth implementing, I think that understanding the benefits of technology could be explored across a multitude of scenarios.
